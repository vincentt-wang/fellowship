The Mission

The invisible college backs you before anyone competes to.

By the time a founder is fundable, everyone wants in. The Fellowship exists for the stage before that, when the work is real but the market has not noticed yet. We show up as the first believer, with room, capital, mentorship and a sharp evaluation lens, and we compound from there.

Credit where it's due

The model borrows openly from Peter Thiel's Thiel Fellowship and the 1517 Fund's idea of an “invisible college”: back talented builders directly, outside the traditional gatekeepers, and be the first believer before the capital markets ever compete for them.

FAQ

Questions before you apply.

Who is the Fellowship for?
Builders who are already shipping something real: a prototype, early users or working code, and who want to make the leap to founder. We back talent over credentials, so the school, title or network on your CV is not what we screen on.
How long is it, and when does Batch 1 run?
Four weeks. Batch 1 meets on four Saturdays: Aug 15, Aug 22, Aug 29 and Sep 5, then graduates at Demo Day on Sep 9 at the BobaTech Summit.
Is there a fixed curriculum?
No. In the spirit of Founders Inc, there is no set syllabus. Each week pairs you with a flight mentor and pushes your one real project forward, measured by an honest founder update and a single goal for the next week.
What is the Deal Lens evaluation?
Deal Lens is the D/F/V framework we use to read every project: Desirability (does anyone want it), Feasibility (can you build it), Viability (does it compound). Before Demo Day, every founder gets an investor-grade read on where they stand.
Where does Demo Day happen?
On the BobaTech Summit stage, Sep 9. The summit is hosted by Stanford ASES Taiwan and is Asia's first Gen Z founder summit, with speakers including Kevin Lin (Twitch co-founder) and K.S. Pua (Phison founder).
How do I apply?
Hit any Apply button on this page and tell us what you are building. Batch 1 is forming now and reviewed on a rolling basis, so earlier applications get earlier decisions.
