The Mission

The invisible college backs you before anyone competes to.

By the time a founder is fundable, everyone wants in. The Fellowship exists for the stage before that, when the work is real but the market has not noticed yet. We show up at the earliest stage, with room, mentors, an honest evaluation lens and a stage at Demo Day, and we compound from there.

Credit where it's due

The model borrows openly from Peter Thiel's Thiel Fellowship and the 1517 Fund's idea of an “invisible college”: back talented builders directly, outside the traditional gatekeepers, at the earliest stage of all.

The Model

This model is not a bet. The best programs already proved it.

Community first, capital early, selection by performance rather than a polished pitch. The numbers below are from the programs that built this playbook.

Founders Inc
San Francisco · invite-only campus
$100–250K in, no pitch

You join the campus first, then take investment for roughly 4–7%. Over 100 companies in three years; portfolio company LiveKit reached a $1B valuation.

Berkeley SkyDeck
University accelerator
Demo Day to 900+ investors

Around 20 teams a batch. Over 1,500 companies to date, alumni have raised more than $1.7B, and it incubated Lime. Being selected is itself the signal.

South Park Commons
Community-led fund
$400K on selection, $600K reserved

Invests $400K when you join and reserves $600K for the next round. Over 800 alumni, with one partner for roughly every two companies.

Founder University
Train, then invest
$500 refundable, then $25–125K

A 12-week program with refundable tuition. It invests $25K–125K in the strongest graduates, then routes them into an accelerator. Performance decides, not a pitch.

Antler
Day Zero · gather first
1,400+ companies, 3 unicorns

Gathers talent before the team even exists, then forms companies from the room. Named the most active venture investor globally in 2024.

Thiel / 1517
The invisible college
5.9% billion-dollar rate

Backing talent at the earliest stage: a 5.9% billion-dollar founder rate versus roughly 1.9% for YC, and $750B+ in ecosystem value. The lineage this program follows.

One honest note on the numbers above: those are the programs we learn from. Batch 1 itself is mentorship and a stage at Demo Day, not an investment round. The capital layer comes as the line proves out.

The counter-lesson: On Deck built 1,000+ companies in four years, but tried to do everything at once, lost focus and had to cut back hard. So we stay narrow: one small batch a year, depth before scale.

Sources: f.inc, skydeck.berkeley.edu, South Park Commons, founder.university, Antler, Dealroom, Specter (n=185), PitchBook, TechCrunch. Some equity and batch figures are industry-compiled estimates.

How it works · 4 weeks

One flight mentor a week. One honest founder update. One goal for the next.

FAQ

Questions before you apply.

Who is the Fellowship for?
Builders who are already shipping something real: a prototype, early users or working code, and who want to make the leap to founder. Most are pre-seed, often Gen Z, with a product or real results and usually little revenue yet. We back talent over credentials, so the school, title or network on your CV is not what we screen on.
How long is it, and when does Batch 1 run?
Four weeks. Batch 1 meets on four Saturdays: Aug 15, Aug 22, Aug 29 and Sep 5, then graduates at Demo Day on Sep 9 at the BobaTech Summit.
Is there a fixed curriculum?
No. In the spirit of Founders Inc, there is no set syllabus. Each week pairs you with a flight mentor and pushes your one real project forward, measured by an honest founder update and a single goal for the next week.
What is the Deal Lens evaluation?
Deal Lens is the D/F/V framework we use to read every project: Desirability (does anyone want it), Feasibility (can you build it), Viability (does it compound). Before Demo Day, every founder gets an investor-grade read on where they stand.
Where does Demo Day happen?
On the BobaTech Summit stage, Sep 9. The summit is hosted by Stanford ASES Taiwan and is Asia's largest Gen Z founder summit, with speakers including Kevin Lin (Twitch co-founder) and K.S. Pua (Phison founder).
Is Batch 1 open to the public?
Not this time. Batch 1 is a small, invitation-only group of 10 to 15 teams drawn from the host's founder network, and Demo Day is invitation-only too. Register your interest and we will keep you in mind as places and future batches open up.
How do I register my interest?
Use any Register interest button on this page and tell us what you are building. We read every note. If there is a fit for this batch or the next one, we will reach out.
